Basic Case:
Equivalence Class: A case where i and j are the same.
Example: algorithm(5, 5)

Small Range:
Equivalence Class: A small range of integers where j - i is relatively small.
Example: algorithm(10, 15)

Large Range:
Equivalence Class: A large range of integers where j - i is relatively large.
Example: algorithm(1, 1000)

Single Number:
Equivalence Class: i and j representing a single number.
Example: algorithm(50, 50)

Even and Odd Numbers:
Equivalence Class: One or both of i and j being even or odd.
Examples: algorithm(8, 15) or algorithm(7, 14)

Negative Numbers:
Equivalence Class: Negative values for i and j.
Example: algorithm(-10, -5)

Zero:
Equivalence Class: i and j being zero.
Example: algorithm(0, 0)

Mixed Signs:
Equivalence Class: i and j having different signs.
Example: algorithm(-5, 5)

Edge Cases:
Equivalence Class: Special cases that may involve the boundaries of the input space.
Examples: algorithm(Integer.MAX_VALUE - 1, Integer.MAX_VALUE) or algorithm(Integer.MIN_VALUE, Integer.MIN_VALUE + 1)
